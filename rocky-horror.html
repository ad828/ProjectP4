The Rocky Horror Picture Show

The Rocky Horror Picture Show

A newly engaged couple’s car breaks down, and winds up meeting Dr. Frank -n- Furter, a bizarre alien who seeks to create his “Rocky”, an unconditional lover that will grant him eternal happiness. Both a parody and a tribute of other midnight double features, this film lives, and breathes passion for the many science fiction films that came before it and defined it. Absolutely panned on the film's initial release, midnight screenings breathed new life into this film's livelihood. This film’s fame is mostly due to the LGBT scene in the 70’s. They were attracted to the film's themes of loving who you are in your own skin, knowing yourself, and not being afraid to try to be adventurous. People gathered in droves at midnight in the 70s to yell the film's lines, throw food and toilet paper at the screen, and “shadow cast” the film above them. More of a social phenomenon than a film, it’s camp hijinks are sure to resonate with anyone watching who wants to forget anything they know and just try to have a good time.
